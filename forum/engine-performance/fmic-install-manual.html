i'd agree on the bracket; in the previous thread there was an additional one added and an innovative support using one of the clamps and a rubber bushing on the other side. One reason for the added bracket was the distance the hot pipe to the fmic travels sideways without more support than a ~7/8"x3" tab to the upper block that was the way the kit arrived. Plus there would be greater latitude for any expansion that may occur with a attachment point further from the flange by the turbo.
There was the additional reason from installers past experience also of having mechanics lean on the top span of the hot i/c tube for support while working on the car's engine; the installer added a vertical brace to the body using a string clamp and a couple slotted pieces of bolted metal for added support. It holds great and i've had one leaky clamp to tighten.
It was recommended to wrap the hot pipe in the area of the exhaust manifold where the pipe actually touches the exhaust heat shield.
