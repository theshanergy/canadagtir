www.nistune.com just read every document they have on their website. its not rocket science. they even have starter guides and manuals on how to tune. i don't see how this is so hard to read up on or search?

they send u a daughterboard u have soldered to your oem computer. you run a cable from the factory consult port that sits in your fuse panel beside the steering wheel. its a usb connector on one end, and a plug for the consult port. you use the nistune software to connect to the ecu, you then can modify the fuel, timing, injectors, maf, yadda yadda. You can also hook up a wideband and it'll accept this feed into the program allowing you to maptrace with afr readings.

your other option is the calumsult. you send your factory ecu to him, he puts a daughterboard on it and sends it back to you. you download the free tunerproRT software and tune with that. It's not as fully integrated and user friendly like the nistune, but its free.

227-170 = 57hp 22.9% loss
330-268 = 62hp 18.9% loss 340-268 = 72hp 21.2% loss
417-328 = 89hp 21.3% loss

your estimation of 330 - 268whp = 62hp (my guess is you were probably making more than 330, probably closer to 340)
it's not going to be a fixed loss, its a % loss that increases as power is increased. which isn't fixed either, the % of total lost falls as well.
