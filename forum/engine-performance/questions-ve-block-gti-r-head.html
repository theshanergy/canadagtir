problem would be getting the transmission and transfercase on, you'd have to go angle grinder happy on block and transfer case

the ve block is a wide block, not a narrow awd block, its got the 4 cw crank and no girdle

tap an oil return

plug vvl oil feeds

tap for water and oil supply for turbo

thread the block to take the larger gtir head studs too

if ur going the route of the ve block , you might as well use the whole motor to take advantage of the vvl head

I see you are front wheel drive so the ve block will fit just fine in your car with no grinding.  If it were me, the gtir head is a waste of time for what it's worth.  If you want a CHEAP build that will make decent power and rev nicely put a highport de head on the ve block.  You will get a high compression de with a 4 counterweight crank so it will rev very nice.  If you get the block for cheap you can probably find a de head for dirt cheap and just slap them together.  Later you could drop some larger cams in easily, and get a header.

The best part is that when people look under the hood they will see a highport sr20de and wonder why it is so quick.

If you wanted, gtir quad throttles have been known to be modded onto highport heads, you could do that and switch to MAP with nismotronic and run some carbon trumpets and be a total baller.  Then you would have a high comp ITB race motor.

 

unless it's the 16ve, then it will be slow....lol
