Hi from England, sorry to butt in, I was just looking for a dash mountable model of the car for mydash when I saw this thread. Nice to see you have some cars over there. Ive owned two now, and the last one for about 8 years now so have learned a few things that I hope will save you guys time and money. In the process of diagnosing my own problems I have ended up with 2 or 3 of everything on the car and now that counts heads blocks transaxles and transfers!

So, high idle. As mentioned above check the screw, it isnt that hard to access just take off the top mount to see it. Close the screw. I would also check all pipes in the system for leaks and breaks. Also a good Idea whilst getting used to the annoyances of access at the back of the manifold (buy a set of bendy necked ratchet spanners, they are a godsend!) you should check the connections of the ficd valve and aac valve, first one is for the air con, the aac is computer controlled by pulses, this is viewable on the monitor software, the ficd valve is opened with the aircon if applicable to you. Both should be shut normally. And opened by the ECU and by the aircon. What isn't viewable on software is lower down at the back is a round valve, the air regulator. They are all throttle bypasses, if the are open then it will idle higher. The air regulator is a heated bi-metallic strip valve that closes as it heats up. This I have seen to leak and not close, I tried to bend the internals but couldn't fix, leaking get another. cheaper to get a bosch equivilent. Test it by taking it off, not fantastically easy I grant you, connecting it up to a battery (12v) for ten mins and seeing if you can blow through it, if you can its passing. Same can be achieved with the aac and ficd, although they are easier, they are also mounted in a common alloy package, three 5mm allen capheads if I remember rightly, it also houses the idle screw. Basically the electric valves are closed, can you blow through them. Be a good idea to clean the out fully too with some carb cleaner/solvent. You will need a new gasket to re assemble, or I got away wih liquid gasket. The 10mm ID pipes that connect all that to the air regulator and eventually the air chamber (black box manifold at the back of the inlet manifold) are hard to remove if original, until you twist them near the ends with pipe grips or plyers then they come off nice, dont be tempted to cut them off, then youll need new ones!

I am trying to save tou going down the road of buying all new valves. By all means I should have said check the obvious throttle being tight buy removing the cable from the bracket. Remember in the software there is a idle speed test, by removing the throttle pot connection timing should fall to 20 degrees tdc and when test started it should idle around 800 - 900. If it doesnt try now forcing the throttle pully home (by hand) if that slows it then the two banks are ill adjusted with the set-screws on the sides, or even the stops grub screws. I messed with all these, still never got it slower than 1200 on mine. Lots of money, lots of disapointment, lots of being pulled round slow corners and junctions!

I obtained some other throttle bodies and solved it once and for all. THE solution is to get a local fabrication/engineering firm to re bore the 4 ports where the butterflies operate, then they make 4 new butterflies and screw them in. Painfull but true, the butterflies cause wear to themselves and to the alloy bores. Once mine were machined the idle is now a beautifully stable 950 under ecu normal control, with varying timing, I think fully warm my aac valve is about 22% open on software. In test mode at 20 degrees timing its high 800s.

I knew a firm and thus was cheap for me, it still shouldn't be too expensive, and will be cheaper than going round the houses and buy all the sensors and valves that I and loads others have done! Not to mentione the heartache and hours wasted! I once asked Nissan a price on the throttles it was multi thousands of pounds, as was an airflow meter, un belivable. This is what we pay for having a one off type of engine that the 54C is, maybe the best and strongest version but they can charge lots for its parts, still I wouldnt cahnge it for the world. Almost sad everyone over here at least ripping them apart each week to see bits on ebay, there will be only a few left in the end, I suggest stock up on bits while you can, we will have to pay full whack one day when they are all gone barring ours!

Hope this helps!
