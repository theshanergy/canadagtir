I would make positive the pcv is operating correctly, if you pull it off, you should be able to put your mouth on the threaded side and blow through it, it should close when you try to suck air back through. If it's not doing exactly that, your probably getting a lot of crankcase pressure. In that case the only outlet is through the oil separator (if it's not blocked) and your intake. If your pressurizing your crankcase, you'll find a hell of a lot more oil in your intake than normal. If the pcv is working perfectly, I would do a compression check. Your maximum should be 164psi for a really healthy engine, and minimum of 135psi for a well run engine (but still runnable.) The pressure between any cylinders should not be greater that 14psi. If your compression numbers don't meet those numbers, you may be getting too much ring blow by, or have a bad head gasket(unlikely with just your symptoms.) If you do in fact get an aftermarket catchcan, make sure that IF you get a vented one with a filter on top, you should block off the oil vent tube going to the intake pipe. If you dont, your maf will detect unmetered air at idle, because it will be pulling it through the catchcan filter. Or just try and get a non vented catch can:)

here's a DIY catch can if you don't feel like spending heaps of money on one!
The hoses should be 16mm.
http://www.pbase.com/rsrock/oil_catch_can

Measure the ID of the hose that goes from the rocker cover to the air intake tube between the air filter and the turbo inlet.

Buy the appropriate fittings and the right size filter from the local hardware store. Assemble and install.

Also, you can block the pipework that exits the back of the rocker cover (by the throttle cable side) by buying the appropriate kit from stevepudney on the gtiroc forum.

Good luck!
