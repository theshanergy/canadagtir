low oil pressure
1. bad oil pressure sensor(for gauge), oil pressure switch(for light) -there's two sensors by the oil filter. I'd check to see if they've been unclipped, or the wires frayed or something.
2. bad oil pressure guage.
3. low oil level.
4. oil pump is going.
5. main bearings are going.
What oil did you use? I'd use a minimum of a 40 weight oil

why not try changing the oil first? Since you're in calgary i'd switch to a 0w40 or 5w40 oil. Try using a 240sx filter. its at least cheaper than what you have planned.

Engine out, rear water hoses (samco sells them), clutch, main bearings (i'd get these checked if you had no oil pressure, then they could be damaged), timing chain and guides, waterpump. If you're planning big power, forged pistons, arp rod bolts, cosworth/tomei/hks headgasket. I guess depends on what you got planned, and how big your wallet is.

For me, this is my daily driver, big power would be great but i just want it to last 2 more years. After that it'll be my winter beater.
