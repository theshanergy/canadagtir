You don't "turn on" the dump valve (bov). it is controlled via a vacuum line from the intake manifold. The oem intake manifold vacuum line to the dump valve is fine, there is no real need to go start t-piece-ing the vacuum nipples (used for calibrating the throttlebodies).

Are you running the oem blow off valve? I can't tell what bov you have on the intake pipe. However, I DO know that you are running a low port sr20. If that dump valve keeps venting to the atmosphere then you will notice the rpm's drop as the engine runs rich for a few seconds after you shift gears or shift to neutral.

the boost leak tester - http://www.vfaq.com/mods/ICtester.html

Not to sound like a pr!ck, but given your engine setup, you would have MUCH much more help over on sr20forum.com

good luck.

you "can" buy a highly modded car, as long as the mods are done right. The headaches that most people run into though are sometimes pretty disheartening.

anyways, there should be a number on the ecu. can you get that for us? At the very least, you may just be out the cost of an air flow meter (something along the lines of a gtir afm (if you dont plan on running it over 14 psi other wise go for a 300zx "z32" afm) and a piggyback air fuel controller like an apexi super air fuel controller 2 (SAFC II) for example that can change the values your stock ecu sees and in turn the stock ecu will still run the car.

other options are to contact jim wolf technology or calumsult on sr20forum.com and ask them what they would charge to reflash your ecu given your mods.

they will probably ask for a list of the mods: fuel system and what size turbo, cams, etc you are running etc so they can tune the map to your needs so the car runs as close to its best as it can.
