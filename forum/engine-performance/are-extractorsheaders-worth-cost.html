he means he is also sold on this shitty ebay one.  Or is showing his support for choosing the shitty ebay one.

Canadian R, I will say this.  Aaron weir makes manifolds for some of the craziest builds I have ever seen.  He makes them to order, they make good power, they look amazing, and they last.  I have never seen a back to back dyno of his manifold vs someone else's manifold, because no one has the time and money to buy several manifolds and dyno them all back to back.  Hell the ebay manifold might make exactly the same power!  But it will eventually crack.  I have 6, ebay sr20det manifolds sitting on a shelf in my garage right now.  ALL of them have cracks in them.  They are all from different sellers, and have different finishes and piping.  They all made great power.  And they all broke in less than one season.  All of the were taken off and re welded, and they all broke again.

Also, log manifolds are not superior to tubular.  They are strong, and cheap, because they are a simple design that is easy to build and package into a tight space.  They can never be superior to a properly designed tubular manifold.  If they were the would be run on every other 4 cyl out there running big power.  I look to the evo crowd when I am curious to know what's what.  They drive 2.0l 4 cyl and make 800+ hp on street cars all day.  I have seen several examples myself at the buschurr race day in Norwalk Ohio.  They ALL use tubular manifolds because they have properly designed runners that time exhaust pulses to make faster spool and best top end power.

Why do you think Subarus gain so much power by switching to equal length exhaust manifolds?  Timed exhaust pulses.
Why do NA cars run tubular manifolds?  They make more power from times exhaust pulses.  The addition of a turbo doesn't change the fact that the exhaust still has to get out of the cylinder to make more power and this happens better when one cylinder isn't trying to stuff exhaust back into the cylinder beside it that still has it's exhaust valve open.

Peg_Pulsar - Okay here's what I did... I bolted the ebay manifold (stock position with a T28) to my block, secured my new turbo (T3 flange) to the turbo to the manifold, took several measurements and pictures explaining exactly what I wanted, then sent him the ebay manifold to make a jig up. All he did to make a jig up for my setup is measure 2 1/4 iches out from the stock flange, tilt the back end out 4 degrees, angle it in towards the block 5 degrees and convert it to a T3... So yes, he can make up tubular manifolds to fit the stock turbo with a T28 flange. And no I don't have my old manifold anymore Aaron does...and its cracked to shite.

Jesse - TBH its a manifold... It has good sized runners, smooth bends and very nice welds. Oh and a lifetime warranty... There isn't very much to them. A quick visual is all you need and you can tell if its going to be putting out big numbers or not.

People go ballistic over 'equal length manifolds', in my opinion they are overrated. Vehicles like Subaru's have the most extreme case of unequal length manifolds and I understand that. But when your runners differ in length by only an inch or less there really isn't a difference. I read an article in a Honda forum a while ago testing the difference between equal length vs 'not quite' equal length. All factors between the two manifolds were the exact same except the manifold with unequal runners differed from 1/4" to 1". The car was dyno'd several times with both manifolds and the total outcome was a 6whp gain from the equal length... For that much of a performance gain I'd rather skip lunch, call it a 'weight reduction', and the outcome would probably be the same on the track... The thing about manifolds that people don't consider are things like the severity of the bends in the runners, WG positioning, runner length, collector design, etc...

Holy fack this thread has blown up. It has gone from "what do I buy!?" to a topic worth discussing.

Hell I'm a huge fan of ebay exhaust headers and manifolds.  They are cheap, shiny, and make power.  But after buying so many of them I would just rather buy a beautiful manifold that is going to last from a manufacturer that is local and does good work; partially because I have the money, and partially because I've learned valuable lessons about cheap manifolds.

Marston, to answer your original question.  Move the battery to the back.  You don't loose that much boot space, you take 30 lbs off the already nose heavy front and add it to the tail heavy rear (basically counts as double in terms of weight bias) and a cold air intake will always help your turbo.  Denser air means more power.  1000 bucks is a waste on the factory turbo.  The GTIR already has the best factory manifold.  If anything I would get it extrude honed and port matched.  I'm sure that would give you a little more peak hp.  But honestly I would only do that if you are going for some kind of record on stock turbo.  The most cost effective way to mod a car for power is to always upgrade the choke point/weakest link.
