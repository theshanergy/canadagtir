When you do go change the pump, make sure to have a low tank of fuel. It makes fishing around for the pump cradle alot easier. Try wearing gloves as the gas will dry your hands out if you have them in the tank as long as I had mine in my tank. (I was an idiot and excited as it was the first mod to the car lol).You'll need 2 butt end connectors for the wires (they may even come in your kit if you are lucky). Some zip ties, etc. Release fuel pressure by pulling the fuel pump fuse while the car is running. It will stall shortly. Try starting it again for a few more cranks then you should be good.

Check for fault codes. If none come up, you can try changing the spark plugs. Some say to get Iridiums, others say to get Platinums. I replaced mine with platinum plugs (cost me $80 shipped for 4, [thanks viz0n]) and the results were awesome. The part number for the platinums (NGK) is PFR7B-9 [insert heat range here]. THe part number for the iridiums is BKR6EIX-11.

run fault codes, replace sensors if needed, replace spark plugs and if you want, throw in a new fuel pump (and filter while you're at it and the pressure is off).

Then take it for a drive and see how she goes. :)

Fast Guy wrote:

Switch ignition on but don't start the engine. In the grey plug under the fuse box, connect the two right pins (chk and ign) with a paper clip for example, for 3 seconds (the engine check light will turn off while you are shorting them out). The engine check light will flash a series which will indicate a code -
Long long long long long (pause) Short short short short short - indicates 55 which is no fault.

If it flashes 3 long and 4 short for example it's the Det sensor.

To clear the ECU codes, short the wires out again while it is flashing the code.

check the wires on the o2 sensor on the down pipe. If you look straight down beween the turbo and the rad, you'll see it stick out of the cast iron down pipe pointing to the front of the car. The wires on mine were fray'ed down to one strand of copper for all three wires as the loom sits very close to the fan shroud right at the back end of the sensor. A new sensor will make a big improvement. If the wires are good, you can test the resistance to diagnose the sensor. If they are bad, replace it. if the resistance is out of spec, replace it. I ordered one from a 1988 300zx TT, go to www.rockauto.com, to get the part number (bosch number 13385) it is a direct fit sensor, not for the plug. You'll have to cut the plug off the old one and solder and heat shrink it to the wires on the new unit. After that, if it WAS your O2 sensor, you should be golden.

All the parts stores did not have this sensor in stock (for the 300zx TT but could order it... it was weeks to get. Nissan could get it quicker from Japan, all of them wanted 100-120 for the unit.

Hope this helps
