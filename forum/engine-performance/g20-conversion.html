As they say:  If you have to ask.......

You will need a welder, angle grinder, drill/drill press, sheet metal brake, tube benders.  Also a space to set the car up, you will need to align the rear sub frame with the front of the car and make your own mounting points for the sub frame.  You will need to move the steering rack and fab a new exhaust.  You will also need to brace the rear of the car as it was never designed to have a pushing force on it or the weight of a sub frame.  A full cage would be ideal but you could get away with some bracing in the right areas.

This is an admirable project.  It has been done...
My position is that I bought a GTiR because it was a lot of car for the money with no huge modification... 
I didn’t have to swap cut or weld... just take care of the known wear and tear items etc and drive it...
For your time and money and living in the sates... I would consider an older evo... 4g63's and their parts for sale on every auto forum... there’s dedicated performance shops.. AMS Meagan racing Sheppard etc...  The GTiR Version of the SR20 DET (54C Block) is a superior motor to the 4g63 but at the cost of well everything convenience wise.
