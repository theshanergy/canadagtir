Here is a note from Peter of GTIROZ:

I'm sending the parcel with three oil adaptors today.
Can I offer some notes to go along with the pieces....

- there is a new Nissan inner o-ring fitted.
- please run some engine oil over the inside of the centre hole and over the o-ring before pressing it into position.
- this is to help force the o-ring over the centre post on the block. It will be a tight fit with the new o-ring.

- the oil-temp sensor port is on top. It does not need any thread-seal, as it seals on the step at the bottom of the thread.
- the loom connector for the temp sensor may require a short extension. This is easily done using spade-connectors and some good gauge wire.

- the pressure-sensor port is on the side (where the factory oil-cooler assembly would be).
- this is a tapered thread (BSPT) and so does require a thread sealant... use gas-tape (PTFE tape) or something like Loctite 542.

- the main oil linel fittings are AN-8.
- use a fitting with an o-ring boss (ORB) seal. This means there is an o-ring at the top of the male thread which squeezes into the chamfer at the top of the female thread.
- examples of ORB fittings can be seen here.. http://www.goodvibesracing.com/orb_fittings.htm
- there is NO JIC-37 or similar facet at the bottom of the threads to seal. Sealing is via the o-ring on an ORB hose fitting.

- Oil will come out of the engine via the lower (outside) port.
- Oil should go back into the engine via the upper (centre) port.

- if your main outside o-ring is not damaged, it can be re-used.
- otherwise, it should be replaced. The Nissan part number is 15239-54C00.
- it is a GTIR-specific part, so may not be readily available from local Nissan dealers.
- there may be a generic replacement available.

- with disconnecting the factory oil cooler, I recommend blocking off both coolant lines.
- rather than looping them. Reducing unnecessary bypass (cold to hot) is a good thing.

Peter
