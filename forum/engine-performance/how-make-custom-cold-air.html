Actually a cold air intake will put a denser air charge into the compressor, meaning more efficient compression and giving more power.  It is also important not to have a large pressure drop inside your intake piping to the turbo inlet.

The harder your turbo has to work the less power your engine will make.

peg, a cold air intake doesn't chill the air.  It places the filter in an area where the ambient temperatures are the lowest possible:  from the outside air flowing around the car.  You don't have to spend $$$ on buying a "cool" pipe.  Anyone can make one by buying some piping and relocating their filter to the fender well, or making a custom airbox to stop hot engine bay air from getting into the engine.
