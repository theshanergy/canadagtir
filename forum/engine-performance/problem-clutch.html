Hi chutez,

I believe your asking about the "clutch disc" which is what you are calling "steering wheel".

I am using a clutch kit from a Nissan S13 with SR20DET. You can use everything but the clutch release bearing which is different. So when you buy the kit you have to order a different clutch release bearing. The SR20DE clutch will work but is smaller in size and will wear out faster and not hold as much power so don't waste your time or money on it when the SR20DET is available.

The clutch release bearing is available through ebay so you shouldn't have any problems finding one.

You will have to use your old flywheel which you should resurface or if you replace it with another SR20DE/DET one you will have to have the crankshaft bolt holes drilled out 1mm larger for the larger bolts the GTI-R crankshaft uses.

The clutch disc can be rebuilt with new discs to replace the old worn out material but that is only 1 part of 5 that you should be worrying about for regular wear.

1. wear on the flywheel surface which is why papa-smurf and I suggested to have it resurfaced for the best wear and contact of a new clutch

2. the clutch disc material which can be re-lined with new material but only at a clutch rebuild shop if parts are available

3. the pressure plate contact surface which also needs to be resurfaced for a smooth and flat contact on the new or rebuilt disc, plus realignment of the disc position compared to the springs so you have the appropriate pressure and disengagement of the clutch disc.

4. Springs on the pressure plate will always get weaker with time and use so the pressure plate springs need to be replaced or the weaker springs may wear out your disc prematurely.

5. Clutch release bearing should be replaced as there is only so much grease/lubrication built into a release bearing for it's entire lifetime. A new one will work much longer then and used ones.

In addition to the clutch you should also check your clutch release for for signs of fatigue or weakness and if there is any have it replaced or reinforced. If the fork fingers, pivot area or slave pine contact areas are worn too much again replacement is best as you will have to do the entire job again is any part of the clutch fails, so spend the money now.

The clutch release for pivot is also something you want to check for excessive wear as this will also effect the ability to have the clutch work right. Also they have been know to break as well and it's a small cost now.

A clutch rebuild shop can rebuild much of the parts if and that is if they have parts to replace them with. Usually they rebuild large truck clutches and our small clutches do not take rebuilding which is why we replace them.

Hope this helps you out.
