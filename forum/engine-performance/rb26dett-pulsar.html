Not going to happen.

RB26 has the front diff housing built into the oil pan. Top to bottom, I'd say the 26 is nearly a half foot taller. I've measured the Pulsar's engine bay, front to back (as it has to be installed longitudinally as the diff runs transverse) it'll fit if you move the rad to the trunk. But, there is no way to attache the trans, as the firewall is near vertical in the GTi-R. The GTR transmission is about 3'long. With the 26 in place, the shifter lever, which hangs off the back of the transmission, would be just behind the arm rest in the GTi-R. Also, you'd need to get, and wire in the Atessa ECU (it's in the trunk on a GTR), the lines and pump, and the fluid reservoir so the AWD would work. That being said, the front axles fit the GTR in the same way as the GTi-R, so you could make Hibrid axles I would say.

Now, for the power stuff. I made 532WHP in my GTR. It cost well over 10 grand to build that. I put over 25 Grand into the engine alone, and it still met it's demise. One of our clients (and a good friend of mine) made just over 520RWHP on a SR20det powered 240. Gt35, 2.2l Crower stroker, nice header, Autronic.

So if it's power your after, build a killer AWD SR. If your after a show/Magazine car, that probably won't run, go for the RB swap!

I went from a 530WHP GTR to a 220HP GTi-R, but I'm still smiling....
