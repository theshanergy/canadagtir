You could get aftermarket guages and a sandwich plate to install them into. That way you can keep the stock oil pressure switche (controls the oil light on the dash) as well as being able to have a much more accurate temperature and pressure reading.

 

I got my stuff from prosport. I would reccomend the sandwich plate - yes the stock nissan filter still fits with the plate installed.

 

I would however, steer you towards autometer or defi guages. Don't go with the prosport. I have to rip my setup apart since the temperature guage reads 0 all the time. :|

 

Factory service manaul says that the minimum oil pressure for the gtir engine is 1 bar at warm idle.

Quoted from the gtiroz site via Smo:

The factory gauge not only lies, but is hideously inaccurate. You're better off (if you can) checking it with an aftermarket oil pressure gauge - standard bottom end oil pressure on the GTi-R engine is just 11-14psi so don't be alarmed if it seems low. My car is a 2.2 stroker with RB26 ACL bearings and my oil pressure when warm is 1.0bar measured with defi gauges.

 

AND:

from the manual:

At Idle = more than 1kg/cm² (varies with oils, and engine condition, but most people report around 2-3)
At 3200rpm = 3.5-4.5kg/cm² (normal operating temp, with no load)
