TWM short shifter

Hey guys,

Wondering if I am the only one who has run into this issue when installing a TWM short shift kit.  

First off, I must say that the instructions included with the shifter were not that usefull.  They were meant for a different car and not a GTIR.  The basics of removing interior panels around the shifter are basic enough that you shouldn't need to be told how to do these steps.  I found it quite difficult to remove the old shifter from the shifter housing once removed.  In the instructions included they reference a circlip which the GTIR does not use.  Even skimming thru our online manual I could not find info apart from an illustrated breakdown of how our shifter assy is put together.  My big issue with this kit is that I had to do some serious modding, not just to the new TWM shifter, but also the shifter linkage as well as the housing for the driveshaft.  I know people had mentioned that the shifter linkage would have to be altered, but am I the only one who had to alter the shifter itself as well as the driveshaft housing?  I found that in order to make this kit fit I had to mod the TWM shifter by removing material around the lower bearing housing. I also had to remove lots of material from the shifter linkage from the gearbox to shifter (which many people had mentioned prior), as well as removing material from the driveshaft upper support?  At the end of a long day at work and five hours later to complete this install, I am very happy with how it feels and drives, but it was by no means a 120 minute drop in install like the instructions had outlined. 

Just some info for others interested in this kit........it is not an easy install like stated in the instructions.

I think adding a spacer under the shifter housing would be a good solution to this problem. You may still have to trim some material off of the TWM shifter itself, but not enough that it would be a concern.  Every other aspect of this kit fits and works, but definately not meant for a GTIR with an AWD setup.

Mat_GTIR, you had asked me how I removed material from the driveshaft housing.....and my answer was VERY carefully.  The housing itself is quite thick metal and all I did was remove a very minimal amount around the radius of it to help aid in clearance.  I never actually changed or removed a section of it, but rather removed a bit of material to change the thickness.  It was a very minimal amount, but without adding a spacer under the shifter housing, this was my solution.
