Battery in the trunk issue, need help!

Ok, so my battery is in the trunk and it's been there for a while now! I've been having issues for a while, but now I really have to do something about it!

Issues (a few years ago): when temperatures dropped, the car would start, but it would be long and hard! lol, you could hear the starter motor turn 3-4 times, then it would stop turning, or would turn slow and eventually car would start. Once or twice, when really cold, the car did not start!

At that point I got a mechanic to check the starter... now in those days no starter here in america was compatible to the GTI-R, lol, I think today someone found a starter motor that fits.... can't remember which... anyways, starter was fine! And if I would place the battery in the car (under glove box at the kill switch) the car would start up fine!

Also, sometimes, when trying to start the car with starter turning at a super slow speed, the fuse would blow!

Before I go any further, here's the set-up:

Optima red top in the trunk, grounded to suspension tower, 4 gage wire to 150A fuse, then routed under carpet, kill switch beside the glove box, out trough firewall to distribution block, starter and alternator connected to distribution block (un fused).

I took my car out yesterday, it started fine all winter (from inside my garage lol) but yesterday, it turned slowly again and blew another 150A fuse!!! Changed the fuse and was able to start.

At this point my car is unreliable, I'm not sure it will start up again... this is annoying!.

I spoke to a few mechanics and I do have a few things I want to check, but I wanted to see if any of you had similar issues, or any suggestions…

- I was told that my power cable could be rubbing against something between the kill switch and the trunk…. I doubt it but maybe… :-\
- Might be a faulty connection at the starter motor, power or ground
- Could be bad ground of the starter
- Maybe 4 gage wire too small, adding too much resistance…

I’m confused as to why is the fuse blowing? If a fuse blows, it’s too much voltage??? Or too much resistance???

Step one, I’ll check the wiring (power and ground) to the starter.

Any ideas or comments are welcome, thanks guys!
