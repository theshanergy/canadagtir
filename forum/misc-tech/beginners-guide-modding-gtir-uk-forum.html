Beginners Guide To Modding A GTiR From UK Forum

First up, you have to service the R, change the oil and filter, change all other fluids i.e. gearbox, transfer case, diff, radiator, brake fluid (Get good Quality oils!!).

Next comes the fuel filter and plugs - The reason for the full service is to ensure you can be comfortable getting more power knowing that nothing has been neglected and it's running at its optimum condition. If you don't know the history can you be sure that the fuel filter has been replaced since the car was made?!! Probably not!!

Most people want to see an instant increase in power, but if you haven't got the brakes or suspension in place – You will not be able to use the power to the full potential!!... So just for a perfect world scenario we are going to go with suspension first (But if you just want RAW power skip this section)

SUSPENSION:
We would recommend the brakes and suspension get looked at first, get some good brake fluid and pads as these make a massive difference to the confidence you have in a car (Especially as the standard brakes are shite). The suspension should cost as much as you can afford, even if you only do lowering springs and a Front Strut Brace it's better than nothing. If you can go full coil over then do it as you shouldn't have to revisit the suspension at a later date.

I would put my money on a car with better suspension and brakes winning a race through a windy course, than the same car with an extra 100hp and no suspension or brake mods. I can safely say a car that can handle well is a hell of allot more fun to drive.

ENGINE MODS:
Ok so if you want just pure power or have already got the suspension set up, then we will start with the power mods!

Really the first things you should do to get good power quickly is fit a full decat turbo back exhaust (Mongoose/Blueflame are most popular), and an induction kit. You will notice a large increase with just these two mods, and you can expect up to a 30BHP gain.

Next people tend to up the boost… But when doing this I would recommend that an up rated fuel pump is fitted first, just so you can feed your engine with the amount of fuel it will need when upping the boost. You can either get a bleed valve or Electronic boost controller, we recommend the controller although they can be expensive and its best to get it set up by someone who knows what they are doing!! With running at about 1bar, and with the filter & exhaust you should expect to see around 275BHP…. Although at this power, you should start saving your pennies as your clutch will sooner of later give up and an up rated one will need to be fitted!

Next your main thought should be a front mounted intercooler, this is quite a large job and requires a lot of cutting and moving – There are several different kits but most people tend to go for either the Hybrid Kit or a Norris Designs one.

BUT depending on how far you want to go (or how deep your pockets are!), the sky is the limit with a GTIR…. Currently some GTIR owners are pushing the 600BHP figures!!
