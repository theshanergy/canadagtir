Battery Relocation

It was easy. If I had to do it again it would only take me half the time and half again the third time.  As it was it took me 2 hours to mount a new box in the trunk, 6 hours to tear the car apart and run the wire and about 2 hours to put everything back.  I also tried to clean out the fan (making noise) but when I tried to pull the motor i had to take out the abs unit, but to take that out i would have to remove a bracket, and to take that out, etc...!  I also took a lot of stuff off that I didn't need to.
In the trunk is the Moroso battery box, sealed with a vent tube.
I bought 20 feet of 1 gauge automotive red wire.
At the battery I used a fancy LED read out terminal with 4 outs (1x1 gauge, 1x4 and 2x8)
A six foot pre-made (crappy tire) blue negative.
Up front I used a terminal block and cut all the wires in the bay and hooked them all up.
I also have a 100amp fuse with holder and a cut off switch with a big red removable key.
I think I'll dash mount the shutoff.
The positive ended up being run through the ECU grommet with a little help from Vaseline.

post your questions and comments.

Ok, drove around a bit and took a look at all the connections.  Everything seemed ok.
Went to the mall for a haircut and when I tried to start the car...NOTHING!
Ok, no biggie, checked under the hood, looks ok.
Check the battery connections in the trunk...dam...need a philips to open the battery box.
Go into the hardware store at the mall (thank god there was one there) and buy a philips.
Open the box and sure enough the positive terminal had come loose.
I bang it down as best I can and head home.
I loosen the positive connector and pry it apart as much as possible (it has a fancy digital display so I am careful with this).
I push it down WHILE prying apart and it sits nice and snug.
The 'problem' is that the hole I drilled for the positive wire is too high and puts constant 'up force' on the connector.
Solution? A scrap piece of wood (cedar shim if you must know) under the battery.
I start to tighten the positive connector and the allen key slips out of my hand and touches both the hold down bracket AND the positive connector.  *ZAP*  Holy crap that scared the hell out of me.  There are now a couple of nice 'weld' spots on my allen key.

Lessons learned.
1) the philips screwdriver I bought now stays in the car.
2) find a way to insulate the hold down bolts from the rest of the car.  Right now they are connected to ground.  If they were insulated then it would be safer to work in the battery box.
3) keep spare underwear in the car until the holddowns are insulated.

8:1 BATTERIES
All batteries must be securely mounted. Must be of sufficient
capacity to start vehicle at any time. Batteries may not be relocated
into the driver or passenger compartments. Rear firewall of .024-
inch steel or .032-inch aluminum (including package tray) required
when battery is relocated in trunk. In lieu of rear firewall, battery
may be located in a sealed .024-inch-steel, .032-inch-aluminum, or
NHRA-accepted poly box. If sealed box is used in lieu of rear
firewall, box may not be used to secure battery and must be
vented outside of body. Relocated battery(s) must be fastened to
frame or frame structure with a minimum of two 3/8-inch-diameter
bolts. (“J” hooks prohibited or must have open end welded shut.)
OEM-located batteries without complete OEM hold-down hardware
must be secured to OEM battery box/tray using the same 3/8-inchdiameter-
bolt hold-down method described in previous sentence.
Metal battery hold-down straps mandatory. Strapping tape
prohibited.
