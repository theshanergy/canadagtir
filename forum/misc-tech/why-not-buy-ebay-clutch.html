Why not to buy an Ebay Clutch.

For those who read the title and said to yourselves "duh", hear me out.

I owned a 93 nx2000 for 2 years.  I ended up supercharging a Jspec sr20 and hitting a 14.0 1/4 in it, with a very rough base tune and only 7 lbs of boost.  I ran an ebay f1 6 puck clutch kit in that beast for 2 years, with over 100 launches, and several autox events.  It held higher than stock power, grabbed hard, and felt great to drive.

So naturally when I purchased my GTiR and did my first tranny swap, I bought the same clutch kit for the R.  Again it held the power, grabbed hard, and was flawless until I had to swap my trans again because the input shaft bearing had blown and taken out the input shaft seal and sprayed gear oil all over the clutch.

So I bought another f1 6 puck disc to replace the one that was saturated in gear oil.  (I have a point to this story trust me

The last f1 disc I put in had a total of 18000 km on it up until last week when suddenly my car wouldn't go into gear while driving home from work.  I figured it was another trans gone.  I cried.  Then I shut the engine off, and it would go into gear.  So I was fairly certain that the clutch had disintegrated.  I cried again because I knew it would still mean that I was taking off the transmission for the 4th time in 3 years.

What I found when I took the trans off was something else:

[IMG]http://i203.photobucket.com/albums/aa126/MrLynx17/GTIR/DSCN1745.jpg[/IMG]
[IMG]http://i203.photobucket.com/albums/aa126/MrLynx17/GTIR/DSCN1749.jpg[/IMG]

I expected that.  I knew that throwing springs was a problem with thee clutches, but chose to ignore it and hope that it would go away.  What I didn't expect was this:

[IMG]http://i203.photobucket.com/albums/aa126/MrLynx17/GTIR/DSCN1746.jpg[/IMG]
[IMG]http://i203.photobucket.com/albums/aa126/MrLynx17/GTIR/DSCN1748.jpg[/IMG]

The clutch had disintegrated.  There was no warning to this, it was still holding power, grabbing super hard, and never slipped once.  I have launched on this thing probably 50-60 times.  8 auto-x events, and plenty of spirited driving.

So as a replacement I spent some money and bought:

[IMG]http://i203.photobucket.com/albums/aa126/MrLynx17/GTIR/DSCN1755.jpg[/IMG]

A real ACT 6 puck.  The quality comparison is non-existant.  The f1 disk is stamped out of the thinnest sheet, which is how the springs are able to wear their way though the small little retaining tabs and get free from their steely prison.  The ACT disc weighs quite a bit more because of the thick disc material.  The springs are also beefier, and the cages holding them in are larger.

I finished the install last night.  Fastest I have ever removed the trans:

[IMG]http://i203.photobucket.com/albums/aa126/MrLynx17/GTIR/DSCN1756.jpg[/IMG]

First time I have taken the trans out without removing the whole engine.  Saved a TON of time.  Had it out in less than 4 hours.  And that was with lots of chatting and distractions.

RESULTS:

OMG! this clutch already feels soooooooo much better.  The HD pressure plate is stff as **** and this thing grabs super hard.  I already stalled it once.  There is no chatter, like with the F1 kit, and the car still shifts great.  Time will tell if the extra money was worth it, but if I don't throw a spring on my way home from work I'll consider it a success.

If you have never done it before, take the engine out.  It will be impossible to remove the trans by itself.

read 

http://www.canadagtir.com/forums/technical/misc-tech/rally-nissan-pulsar-gtir-nme-rnn14-group-n-manual-rally-record
specifically the part about omitting cerain brakets and bolts.  You don't have to do all the modifications they suggest because you are not trying to take the gear out in 10 mins.  Just leave the 2 cruicial bolts out and get rid of the POS Y bracket.

Then just unbolt the tranny from the engine.  Remove the downpipe, and ubolt the driveshaft from the transfercase.  Remove the axles.  Then remove all engine mounts but the drivers side, lower the motor down until you can undo everything behind the motor from the bottom, and then slide the whole trans off.  I did it with 2 people, and only used one jack to keep the motor from falling right down.
