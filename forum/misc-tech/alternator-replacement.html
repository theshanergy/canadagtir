u can use a newer b15 sr20 alternator thats 90 or 100amp which is what i did

my directions takes about 1.5-2.5hrs if you go slow and don't have Air Con, air con is the killer
from what i remember

tools needed
wrenches 8, 10, 12, 14, 17
sockets 10, 12, 14, 17
wobbly
prob a short breaker bar
jack, stands

remove the rad fans
remove the rad fluid bottle
loosen pivot bolt
remove the entire alternator tensioner
remove belt
undo the clip and ground from alternator
now the alternator should be loose and swing up n down
let the alternator drop all the way down, you should see a single 14 or 15mm bolt or nut, undo that, kind of a pain to get at, if you have a small wobbly it'll undo with a short breaker bar if its on tight
jack up the car
use a wooden block or friend to hold the alternator up
there should be 2 - 12mm or 14mm or 15mm nuts, loosen those off, may need help of breaker bar
now u should be able to pull the pivot bolt out back and drop the entire alternator and bracket out the bottom.
install is reverse.

i didn't try removing the other side of the pivot bolt bracket, as it as on there tight, i was too chicken to snap it off, if you can get that off it'll prob come out even quicker

drop to ground
