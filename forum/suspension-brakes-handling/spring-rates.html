i can't give you a real world experience answer regarding those higher rates on a gtir but from my experience

setup:

  1. gtir in autox with 7kg front 6kg rear,
  2. whiteline rear bar at full stiff,
  3. rear damping at f31/32, front at 19/32,
  4. max camber that the camber plates will allow without having to cut the hole,
  5. front poly,
  6. anti lift kit,
  7. pineapples,
  8. 0 toe
  9. Re11 205/50/15 15x7 +30 ( with 5mm plates)
  10. tower bars front n rear

I have witnessed pretty significant outer edge tire wear (from positive camber gain on roll) even with tires pumped up to 44 fron 40 rear

its slow through transitions and hurts bad on anything with any remote amount of lock and speed as the tires are talking WOERRRRRRRRRRRRRR

bumpsteer isn't so bad kuz the pad i'm on is relatively smooth

the gtir sucks hard on tight fast transitions, decreasing radius sweepers, sweepers, but awesome on a straight and if its wet out

 

Things I would want:

Tires:

205 isn't enough tire width, i'd say 225 is minimum and if ur going to cut fenders 255 if you can.

Spring:

i would want at least 12-14kg (700lb-850lb) up front with 16/17 (900lb-1100lb) rear, to help with faster transition and minimizing roll which causes positive camber gain

note this would be just for autox (for rotation and fast sharp transitions), you have differing needs since you u want track duty plus rallyx

Alignment:

if you can dial in a buttload of static neg camber (3deg) to fight off the effects of pos camber gain, it'll help handling a ton ( maybe less camber for track duty )

Damping:

on top of the heavy rate, i would want a revalve with a butt load of low speed damping for transitions, kuz the gtir stinks at a slalom compared to say a miata/crx/s2000

 

The above is coupled with my experience in a crx with 550-650lb rates in a 1930lb car that i am easily setting times 2-4 seconds faster depending on the track setup than my own gtir.  it only has 110hp and open diff to put it into perspective.  it has also been cornerweighted and properly aligned with setup tips from the man who drives god's chariot , if ur a big autox'r u know who this is.  the guy i rent my drive from is friends with him.

forgot to mention the bc racing coils seem to have poor low speed damping, high speed damping is fine, but its quite evident when ur working through a slalom how much the gtir squats side to side,and how slow it is to transfer that weight

 

also tire suggestions : (i have driven on dunlop z2's, dunlop star specs, toyo r1r's, bstone re11's, bfg rivals, advan a032r's, toyo r888's in an autox environment)

For street tire classes above 140aa tw

wet only : toyo r1r - in the summer they get way too hot and need watering between runs

dry only or damp: bfg Rivals - i have to say i am most impressed by this new tire, super super forgiving, lets u slide and manhandle, and it won't snap u into a spin, back on throttle and it pulls u back gently, barely wears and only gets stickier when hot, my fave tire at the moment.  it also barks and talks a lot to give u a heads up.
