All other things being equal settings should return to the same in springtime; perhaps a wheel alignment might be worthwhile the meantime.

You might want to google: ___whiteline KCA4___ and get the pdf file "Z201_KCA4(size)" which can be had at the top of the list.

"KCA410, KCA412, KCA414, KCA415, KCA416, KCA417
Application:
KCA4(size) is a universal fitment adjustable camber bolt kit and is designed to provide up to +/- 1.5 degrees of camber adjustment using a special high tensile eccentric bolt to replace upper strut clevis bolt. They can also be used to replace the lower bolt for additional positive or negative camber but only for race applications."

The ones that were loose in the glovebox of my gtir are labeled KCA412.

I'm using some Michelin X-ice, which are nice for grip and good ride.
Size i'm running is 185/60x15 on alloy rims. They don't rub and it runs a bit taller.

I had 195/60x15 M&S for about 1000 miles last summer, but they rubbed slightly on bumps and hard turns and were replaced.

The overall diameter of your tires are too big, it is simply your tire rubbing against the plastic inner lining when turning full lock. Same thing was happening when I was wearing some 205-50-16 last year. Unfortunately it is not something an alignment will not cure. As for the handling not being tight, it can be directly attributed to the cushy and compliant sidewall of your winter tire which is most likely an H or T speed rated tire.

p.s. negative camber is great all summer and winter long, no need to change it.
