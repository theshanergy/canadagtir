u do that. you'll probably get 0 response to what big brake kit fits under a 14" rim

you're pretty much at the limit of fitment under the stock 14' wheel unless you run a fat ass spacer with extended studs. which would cost u at least 150$ even then you'd have to have a custom brake kit built.

at the end of the day its cheaper to just put winter tires on your 15's and use an off the shelf prebuilt 11-11.75" brake kit.
15" winter tires are at most 50-70$ for a full set more expensive than 14" winter tires.

but sounds like u don't have the money, then just get a pair of good pads and fluid. carbotech's and dot5.1 fluid will run u around 15-200$
else hawk hp+ or hps and fluid will run u around 100$ in any case, if you're running a shitty summer tire, your tires will play as big as a role as your pads will.

putting on a brake kit would put u into a different class or add so many points that it would put u into an SM class neways.
i dont know what rules are followed in your area but take a look at this http://www.scca.com/contentpage.aspx?content=61

i would just change your brake fluid to either motul, ate, or castrol dot 5.1
run hawk hp+ or blues in front, and hawk hps pads in rear.
have your rotors machined, or pick up another set for the front.

that will cost u in the neighborhood of 200-300$.

hp+ and blues will eventually eat up your rotors up front, so factor that into your costs.

i'd also try to figure out a way to run some small diameter aluminum dryer hose duct it from the front to the rear of the rotors to keep the fronts cool.

removing the a/c,
draining your windshield washer bottle,
removing as much as u can from the interior to the point where its not considered removing interior for points,
removing all the underhood shrouding by the scoop,
running the lightest possible wheel and stickiest tire,
change the battery to an odyssey pc680 and relocate the battery or moving the battery under the intake hose so its closer to the centre of the car.
will all help your braking ability.

your worst enemy is weight.
