Yes, i experienced this with my gti-r. Do you have stock rubber lines still? If not get a set of stainless lines, it'll help a bit. However, you're simply suffering from brake fade and boiling the fluid. The stock brakes are garbage for the heavy gti-r. They'll fade after 1 or 2 hard stops and they're done. After nearly rear ending someone on the highway, i changed the brakes. Now my problem is trying to keep the car off abs.. hehe. Literally a night and day difference. I wouldn't bother with a power mod before this.

Just run 15's with winter tires. That's what i have. Use a cheap set of rims lke rota slipstreams. They'll fit over a 11.75 kit from fastbrakes or the 11 inch kit.

These kits come with wilwood 4piston dynalites, wishbone to move the caliper out, rotor, hawk pads, and stainless lines. I paid 650 for mine. Plus 50$ roughly in taxes.

I have the 11 inch fastbrakes wilwood kit with 2 sets of wheels. Some ssr knock off's in 15x6.5 plus 40 offset with 185/60/15's for winter tires. My summer wheels are enkei rally es-tarmac's in 15x7 plus 40 offset with azenis in 205/50/15's.

Well i experienced the same issue as you did. I machined the rotors, new fluid, new pads, stainless lines. Bed them in properly and started to brake hard again. Same problem. Simply the brakes are not big enough for the car.

Anyways how do you know if the brakes are not hot? The fluid could be boiling, the rubber lines expanding, hence the mushy pedal feel.

Anyways you can test this. Accelerate slowly to 100km/hr and stand on the brakes, do this 3 times. Then let them cool off. Accelerate quickly to 100/hr and stand on the brakes, again 3 times. I guarantee the brakes will feel shitty either way. With a nearly 70 / 30 front to rear weight distribution, the 10inch brakes are way too freaking small.

Anyways, I changed to the 11 inch discs and the problem went away instantly.

If you ever come to Vancouver, contact me. I'll stand on the brakes for you, and you'll change your mind. My bestfriend sat in the passenger seat and asked me to stand on the brakes at 50km/hr. He missed the dashboard by a 1/2inch.
