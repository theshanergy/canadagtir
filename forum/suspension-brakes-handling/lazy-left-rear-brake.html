Section BR38 at around page 1800 in the nissan repair manual details the r&r fpr the rear disk brake caliper piston removal procedure if needed, and it seems relatively straightforward to DIY. You could do it yourself with medium mechanical skills and the manual likely.

http://www.gtirworkshopmanual.com/default.aspx?v=1800

A set of needle nose pliers to twist the pistons anticlockwise, maybe some snap ring removers and safety glasses, and a couple screwdrivers or picks to remove the seals would be needed as tools if you needed replace the piston. I wasted time not reading the manual; i didn't know enough to twist the pistons to move up or down unlike my german cars, go figure! lol

Maybe twisting it left and right back and forth might get yours moving freely again, but then it may then leak if it's scored or rusted in the cylinder and you have to repair it anyways later.

I've not up on pricing new, but at a guess you'll save tons on a rebuild kit or caliper.If the kits still can be had via a parts jobber with usdm nissan parts lists, all the better. I may be looking at a rebuild myself as brakes likely due in the springtime on mine.

The rear calipers are the integral park brake type and servicing them was one of the first things I did to mine when I got it. It's also a good idea to use a small pocket screwdriver to break the dust boot loose too so it doesn't become twisted with the turning of the piston. Some manufacturers make a special tool to fit in the divots of the piston. Also make sure the divot lines up with the dowel on the pad before reinstalling them.
