Installing a third brake light

Hi there everyone,

In order to be less hassled by the police and save some $ because of non-conformity I have decided to install a third brake light on my car which is mandatory in Quebec. I've been looking for one for quite a while and could not find anything cheap, and good looking.

I've finally came across a 32 LED module on eBay, for 8$ delivered!

You can find it here:
[url]http://shop.ebay.ca/sis.html?_kw=32-LED+Red+12V+Car+Third+Brake%2FStop+Warning+Light+NEW&_id=270706793411[/url]

This is what it looks like:

[img]http://www.vizi0n.com/pulsar/mods/pulsar%20gtir%20-%20mod%20-%20third%20brake%20light%2001.jpg[/img]
[img]http://www.vizi0n.com/pulsar/mods/pulsar%20gtir%20-%20mod%20-%20third%20brake%20light%2002.jpg[/img]

Installation is pretty simple, the module is glued on the back door using 2 sided foamy tape. You could stick it to the glass but I prefered having it on the metal for a lower fitment. If you install it in the window it will hold pretty well, but if you install it like I did (on the door's metal frame below the glass) you might want to use 3M VHB (very high bond) double sided tape as the one that comes pre-installed is very weak and the module will fall off very easily (and often). You can get VHB tape on eBay for quite cheap (I've paid my 8.5x6.5in sheet 7$ shipped).

Steps to install:

1: Remove the plastic trim on the back door
2: Run a double wire thru the inside of the door frame and exit by the top left rubber tunnel (between the door and ceiling). This part isnt easy or fun.
3: Solder your red and black wires from the module onto the ones you just passed inside the door (Red is +12v, black is negative/ground)
4: Tap your +12v on the Red/Green wire on the rear left tail light and the ground on the black wire. You could also ground it elsewhere, but I prefer having all my wires together.
5: Tie-wrap everything for a snug fit and to make sure your wires dont rub and cause a short circuit
6: Center the module on the door and glue it to the door
7: Align the door's plastic trim and cut a V notch to allow the wires inside, and reinstall the trim
8: Measure your LED module width, height and position, then cut your cargo cover with a skilsaw to allow the module to fit. Mine was 11" wide for 32 LED
9: Test fit, enjoy.
