Last year I ran Kumho STP's for daily and weekend autox.  Ran on 15's as well.  Did ok, but the 320 treadwear was noticeable on the autox.  So far this season I've been running the Federal 595evo's.  At 280 treadwear, they did better on the autox than the STP's, but showed their weakness once the temps got up there as they got greasy when hot.  I'm going to keep them as my daily tire as they work real well for the money on the street.  Looking to run the 615K's on 16" rims for autox, the tall sidewalls of the 15" tires is currently limiting my car on the track.

I ran the following for tires at autocross over the past two year:

Dunlop Star Spec Z1 - 205-50-16
Falken RT-615k 205-40-16

The Dunlop are definitely the stickiest street tire followed closely by the RE-11. However, I found that the 50 profile on the 16 was too tall and opted for a 40 profile last year. The new Falken RT-615k is very close to the Star Spec grip wise but not as good in the rain.

It's too bad the Dunlops are only available in 195-55-15 if you are looking at a set of 15" because it is a clear winner at autocross and lapping events.

Hankook RS-3s are awesome and a very capable tire but the very low thread wear makes them less appealing unless you drive very little.

Stay away from the BF KDWs, the get so hard and greasy.
