Here's a couple pics together of the rear skid plate mounted on my car.

It has taken some damage and suffered rock erosion as seen at the lower right photo.
The rear skid plate is actually a sandwich construction:
two thin layers of aluminum, with a kind of aluminum honeycomb in the middle. Perhaps some resin bonding is holding it together, but i'm not sure.

If you have aftermarket adjustable sway bars and an aftermarket coilover suspension along with a rear skid plate, you may wish to check for interference with the tip of the sway bar and and the rear side edges of the skid plate. This can be seen at the lower left of the image. The swaybar was set full stiff position using aftermarket coilovers and there was hard contact on a couple of occasions each side.

The damage is done now, but if the slackest setting for the swaybar will not need be used, maybe the end of the swaybar could be cut it off for more clearance with the skid plate and aftermarket coils set low or adjust suspension accordingly.

Using Nismo suspension with a Whiteline anti roll bar has produced no contact with the rear skid plate so far.
