For Sale: My Pulsar

Selling my 1991 Nissan Pulsar GTi-R

147,300 km

This was my daily driver for the last two years and I love this car. 

I'm selling it because I need something more practical (wtf, did I really just say that?). I will soon be commuting about 100km daily so I want something that's more comfy and better on gas.

That being said, I have managed to get 500km to a tank with this car before, which is almost unheard of with these. Usually closer to 400, it all depends on how you drive. 

I fixed all the vacuum leaks and boost leaks that were present when I first bought it. I only use full synthetic oil, changed every 6000km. 

I have the car at 7psi, which is LESS boost than factory (10psi). I wanted the car to be as reliable as possible.

Everything electrical works including the power foldable mirrors.

I have replaced a ton of parts in the past two years, including the two biggest question marks: the turbo and the clutch. 

The following maintenance parts have been replaced with brand new items:

- Clutch and pressure plate (replaced with ACT HDSS)

- Turbo (rebuilt with new seals bearings and thrust washers)

- Radiator

- Front rotors and pads
- Rear rotors (impossible to get, Japan only!) and pads

- Front lower control arms incl. new balljoints and bushings

- Distributor cap and rotor

- Ignition coil

- Alternator + belt (replaced 2 months ago)

- Battery (replaced 1 week ago)

- Clutch master cylinder (rebuilt)

- Clutch slave cylinder (rebuilt)

- New clutch soft line (steel braided line)

On top of that, the car has the following upgrades:

- GABsport shocks and springs 

- Hella projector fog lights mated to OEM Nissan switch

- Hella driving lights

- Headlamps replaced with OEM Nissan pieces from europe. LHD european beam pattern throws off better light without blinding anyone

- Clutch pedal reinforcement bracket (a must)

- Apexi Hybrid Megaphone exhaust (another super hard to find item)

- HKS SSQV blow-off valve

- AEM dryflow air filter

The bad:

- Body isn't in the greatest shape. No major rust, but it is forming around the wheel wells and around one of the door sills

- A/C doesn't work

- Needs an alignment

- Floor's been repaired with a steel plate (this was done before I bought the car) under the passenger footbox

- It has the umbrella but the umbrella itself needs to be fixed

This is literally the best car I've ever driven in the snow. It has so much grip in winter it's stupid. 

It also has way more interior room than it should for a car of its size.

Fold the rear seats and it will carry way more stuff than you'd think was possible  - I've been able to fit two S13 seats, a desk, an office chair, etc.

Roof rack not included.

Anyway, enough talk.
