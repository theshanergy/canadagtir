how would this work?

I think you are forgetting that FWD vehicles have ring gears on their front wheel differentials.  Our centre diff is in the front diff location for all nissan FWD vehicles.  Then remember that our centre diff has 2 shafts that connect to the transfercase and the axle goes right through the centre diff in the TRANSMISSION to connect to the front differential in the TRANSFERCASE.  There is no way a FWD LSD will work in our AWD cars without serious modification if at all.

Like AtomicGTiR if this can happen after the Holidays this would work out in favour for me as well. I am interested So please keep me updated to the status of this potential group buy.

As for the Nismo LSD from the Altima it has already been done (check for sale thread "Brand New Nismo Front LSD for Sale" for pics)

VLSD use Viscous fluid to lock up and do wear down but also don't perform the best.
Clutch type are good for racing but need proper set up for your driving style and will wear out clutches and need replacement eventually.

Helical Diffs - don't need any more maintenance then a regular open diff... ie : oil changes and it constantly adjusts pending on torque load
VLSD - Need special fluid replacement inside the unit itself (which means taking it out again) - Must slip first to heat up but also doesn't release the quickest.
Clutch type - Need to be tuned (removed and set-up each time till right) then will need a change of clutches eventually... another removal. Can be set-up to lock in either direction and with varied torque load to lock the diff. Downside = lots of maintenance to get it to an ideal set-up or just regular maintenance alone.
