Good things about it.

-All of the electronics work, including the A/C, sunroof, power mirrors, windows and door locks.
-Still has the umbrella!!!!
-The exterior has no rust. 
-Paint is in very good shape, with no significant dents
-It runs really well. I’ve fixed all the problems I’ve encountered along the way. Other than the starter, none of them have been engine related, and the motor still pulls strong and runs like a clock.
-I also have a set of stock wheels with Goodyear Nordic winter tires (Still have lots of tread)

Bad things about it.
-The front drivers side spring turns back and forth slightly in the top seat when the steering wheel is cranked. This makes a thunk noise that had me worried until I tracked it down. It’s more of an annoyance than anything.
-There is a small grind when shifting into 5th under load. I’ve made a habit of double clutching when going into 5th, which alleviates the problem.
-There are a few rock chips in the hood, and the occasional small ding here and there. The kind of thing to be expected from a 20 year old car.
-There is a slight fade on the paint on the roof, and plastic portion of the hood scoop. 
-There are two small cracks in the dash. I’ve been told it can be fixed, but I haven’t gotten around to it.
-There is a bit of rash on the passenger side skirt near the end. It’s not very noticeable since I used the paint pen on it.

I bought this car from an import place in Calgary a few years ago and registered it in B.C. when we moved out to the island. It has all the necessary upgrades to pass inspection, including third brake light, daytime running lights, and LHD headlights. It’s been registered and insured in B.C. for a year and a half now.
