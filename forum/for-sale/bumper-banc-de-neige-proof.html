Looks like it would work great to fend off the hard snow banks in winter time and might help to deflect the car off the snow banks and bounce it back onto the road in some situations. [fwiw: one ice racer i met uses the snow banks regularly to deflect off of like pool table bumpers as a part of his successful driving technique, though this technique has it's hazards.]

The metal guard bounding off the snowbank would hopefully keep the tires stood off from mounting the snow bank and possibly prevent the car flipping on the roof or getting hung up on the bank somewhere out in the sticks.

I agree more force would be transmitted to other parts in a crash and it's a compromise with damage. Like seam welding to stiffen the unibody in race prepped cars, more force is transferred to other parts down the line and the ultimately to the driver or passengers in any really hard crash. A good 5-point harness and good roll bar or cage might be recommended if going 10/10ths.
Some would want a couple tow hooks and light bar attachment points too as Alex suggests. Best Success!
