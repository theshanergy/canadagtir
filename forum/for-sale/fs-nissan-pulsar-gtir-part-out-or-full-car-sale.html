That's my old Pulsar, so let's tell it like it is:

 

- The driver's side seat backrest crank is slightly stripped, so the backrest slowly reclines. I found I had to re-crank it up to the stop at the beginning of every drive. Drove me a little crazy.

- The drivetrain was in very good shape, up to the accident. Not sure about the shape of the rear differential or axles, etc now. The "light bump" the car took was pretty significant. The rear passenger bearings and hub must be fooked.

- As I've mentioned in other posts, I could never get the engine's idle sorted out properly, even with the help of Jackson Autosport here in Winnipeg, who did a great job of fabricating a 3" turbo elbow to downpipe/ cat delete but couldn't sort out the idle either (I noticed the exhaust is not for sale? Oh well, there was a rub-hole in the pipe after the cat delete pipe). The idle started off ok, then would climb to ~1300 rpm once it warmed up and would sit there unless I put the engine under load (car in gear, slowly let out the clutch to the friction point with my right foot on the brake), then I could "force" it down to ~1000 rpm and it would sit there once I depressed the clutch again.

- The clutch is stock, and started to occasionally/ slightly slip under WOT.

- The A/C worked well, except it bogged the acceleration so bad (compressor bearings?) I only used it on the hottest days in stop-and-go traffic.

- Of course, the hydraulic tailgate strut no longer held the hatch up, especially once the temp got nearer to zero celcius.

- The headlights are JDM

- The interior door panels have tweeter holes cut in them from the speakers I pulled out (along with the after-market stereo).

- There is a Walbro 255 fuel pump in the gas tank.

Most everything else is stock and as expected. It was a good car up until the accident, too bad it can't be driven as a full unit again (except on the track, but doubt that'll happen)
