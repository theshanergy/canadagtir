I went through the instructions for Scotchprint. It specifies a cleaner and then a solvent cleaner. I used a cleaner and my heat gun to blow off debris and to make sure no oils were on the surface. I used latex gloves rather than cotton as specified, and the original surface does not have paint or primer on it, just plastic.

It was quite difficult as a first attempt and I was tired after the 2 hours or so I spent manipulating the material. I split the application into 2 portions, left and right. The heat gun really helps when you get yourself in trouble.

I'm hiding a lot of mistakes lol

Air bubbles got the best of me and I spent a lot of time trying to manipulate them out. Especially on contour areas.
I'm sure if I am dissatisfied enough, I will redo. it will most likely happen lol.
