[img]http://www.canadagtir.com/sites/default/files/old/gallery/100_3163.JPG[/img]

I also got a Cusco lower brace from nick at the same time.

[img]http://www.canadagtir.com/sites/default/files/old/gallery/100_3215.JPG[/img]

Next on the agenda was rollers for the snow. It took little time at all to decide on wheels. With the WRC heritage of the car, OZ's were near mandatory!! 15" Suppertourismo's, wrapped in Kumho I*pike's:

[img]http://www.canadagtir.com/sites/default/files/old/gallery/DSC03550.JPG[/img]

I have a set of JIC's on the way to address this wheel gap, and the fact that the front OEM coils are leaking pretty badly..

[img]http://www.canadagtir.com/sites/default/files/old/gallery/DSC03549.JPG[/img]

Also in the near future, I have a FMIC from my old GTR to install. AC will be deleted. Battery is headed to the trunk. Custom I/C and intake pipework. AVC-R to install, LC-1 wideband and a Vipec full stand alone EMS....

i'm also glad the superpro bushings worked out, i have to say the anti lift/castor bush and the rear subframe lock and align bushes are the most effective on the whole car. If you were on a budget, those would be the two to do. I would suggest a bumpsteer kit and the whiteline adjustable rear sway bar to finish off the suspension. that will clear the understeer as much as you possibly can. Of course you can also get the whiteline front swaybar as well as its 1mm smaller in diameter, but if you're on a budget i would do it in that order. 1. antilift castor bush 2. rear subframe lock and align 3. whiteline rear sway bar 4. bumpsteer kit 5. coilovers 6. the rest of bushings
