I'm certain a good wheel shop should be able to install painted rims carefully and they'll look great; but the paint will wear off at the bead where there is contact. Hopefully not an issue and powder coating would be great.

I can really see the reason for brightly colored rims in winter. I had s yellow set and a red set for my little mitsu based ice race car.

Rather than a vivid contrast in wheels and body color, I recently had some rims paint-matched 'twice' on another jdm vehicle i own. Second time painting the same wheels because of a subtle color difference with the whole car's respray. Close but no cigar using two different companies and the same paint code
[Most of the problem matching was getting the actual formula for the japanese paint code in NA itself]

There were two approaches to painting the wheels:
1.
The wheelwrights who repaired and trued the rims, painted them without masking off the wheel prior to respray. They do it that way all the time no probs. Powdercoating's recommended, but i wasn't convinced it would accurately match body color or luster, so a special mix of rattlecan paint was applied in many light layers.
2.
A large auto factory associated bodyshop where my jdm car was later being painted, masked the wheel off [as below] prior to their respray. The wheels were painted along with the rest of the trim like mirrors and door handles. They noted the previous over-spray from the wheelwrights job but said it shouldn't be a big problem.

Nonetheless, the paint shop always mask off their wheel painting jobs, [using bleed resistant and water resistant paper wrap]. They're assured of no possible problems with any leaks at the bead attributed to the paint job. No doubt leaks could occur, so better safe than sorry.

fwiw: ... the cost of the job was fifty cdn per wheel extra to get paint at the bodyshop as part of complete re-spray using a computerized color matching process and the same paint as the car. Wheelwright charged only thirty bucks extra for the paint. Both coverings looked great.

i hope that helps ...
