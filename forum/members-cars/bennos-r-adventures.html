Benno's R adventures

So even though I don't currently have a GTi-R I figured I would document this process from the start. My projections of getting an R in late November / early December are looking good. I've spoken to insurance and they say it is a go. Quoted $250/month with collision though so I might ask around a little. Not super expensive, about $60/month more than my 98 Civic but worth looking into. I am with State Farm. Also thought that it would probably be a good idea to start checking some of them out. Since I have only ever seen one GTi-R in person I still need to lose my R-ginity and take one for a spin. Contacted both Right Drive and Tiger to arrange a visit. I have a test drive booked with Right Drive, not sure if Tiger does those. Either way it should be a perfect way to see a great example (Right Drive) vs a potentially crappy example (Tiger). So things to look out for?? Compression should be around 150-160psi? Excessive timing chain noise. Rattling struts. No hacked wiring or signs of a boost controller = rape-age.......... then the usual driving / braking in a straight line. Normal tire wear = good alignment. A/C works or deleted. So if anyone here wants to sell their car before winter then let me know. I'll be looking to buy in 6-8 weeks.

Alright so I guess I need to update since I finally got to see a couple in person yesterday. Me and my bro drove down to Right Drive in the morning. They were the only test drive I had booked but figured it was a safe bet I would get a good idea of how a proper one should drive. Unfortunately I was I wrong about that one. The main problem being that the clutch was completely pooched so it would max out at about 60km/h and take 20 seconds to get there :-( There were other problems with the car but I will spare you the details, needless to say we were very disappointed. So next we drove over to Tiger to see the supposed "crappy" cars as some point out. We were actually quite impressed. Maybe it was the let down of the Right Drive car but the Tiger cars fired up and seemed to be running great. I wasn't able to get in a test drive, just a roll around the parking lot, but besides a few minor points (mostly interior cosmetics) they seemed good. So in about a month I will go shopping. The plan is do road trip to Quebec to check out a couple and bring one back if I like it. I'm liking the looks of the one at Sunrise JDM but am not going to buy until I find a good one, I am willing to wait. I was very impressed by what I saw though so I did go ahead and order my plates [img]http://i426.photobucket.com/albums/pp342/SirBenno/Myplates.jpg[/img] Anyways I am glad I lost my R-ginity even though it wasn't quite what I had expected or hoped. OK I suppose I should update what has been going on.
I bought an R about a month ago. It has been nothing but good times since then. I've done a few small things like wires, plugs, rotor, cap, replaced the jdm radio, HKS SSQV 3.
The car I bought had 99,000km's on it (Now has 102,000km). I now have my snow tires on it and am looking forward to doing some donuts since it has finally started to snow. My R has a pretty well perfect interior and came with a few extra bits. K&N intake, ARC intercooler, lowering springs, some crazy loud straight back exhaust. It runs and idles amazingly well so I will be taking it down to South Carolina with me for the 9 weeks I spend there in March/April. Ordering some bronze 16" Rota Grids and sticky tires for some fun on the mountain roads.
