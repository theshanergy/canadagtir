Its kuz u have a tiny rear sway bar, not enough psi in the tires in the rear, too much slop in the rear subframe, crappy tie rod ends.

Get a whiteline adjustable 20mm bar, bump up the rear psi to about 36 or 38psi, and get the whiteline subframe align and lock bushings. These 2 whiteline items really transforms the car. A spherical rod end will also help with understeer. You can see the gtiroc forum about bumpsteer kits.

You also need to induce the car into a slide by upsetting the balance and transfering the weight, enter the corner with moderate speed, get on the throttle and give it a quick flick of countersteer while staying on the gas. it'll give it plenty of slide.

Also dialing the rear suspension to a firmer setting than in the front will also help with a more neutral feel.

You should do some more reading up on how to set up suspension.

Anyways read through these articles here as it points out the inherent problems with the gti-r suspension http://www.gtiroz.net/gallery/whitelinehandling

no_boost wrote:
i have really good suspension on my car and i have a very hard time makin the ass end slide. dry wet or snow it doesnt like to slide . but when it does slide it is very easy to snap back in place. and i have strut bars upper and lower front and rear. could that be part a the reason it wont slide.

I'm assuming you have some sort of jdm coilover with some strut bars. This will not fix the understeer issue. Besides strut bars don't do all that much for handling and definitely will not alter the way it handles. Most jdm coilovers come with a front spring that is heavier than a rear spring. This combined with the gti'rs 62/38 weight balance, which is awful, plus the larger front sway bar and skinny rear sway bar equates to suspension designed to understeer.

There are different approaches to fix this, 1 fatter rear sway bar and thinner front bar as whiteline offers. This will reduce the amount of weight transferred side to side up front inducing more roll and grip. The fatter rear bar will transfer more weight reducing roll. This is a similar set up to an integra type R with a large rear roll bar.

Another method is to put some heavy toe out alignment making the car twitchy as hell, however i believe the gti-r suspension design will still understeer.

Another method is to use heavy rear springs in the back, possibly even heavier than the front. This equates to an uncomfortable ride and works similarly to having a larger rear antiroll bar. This is a common setup for fwd autox.

You'll need to study up on suspension setup some more before saying i have really good suspension with strut bars.
