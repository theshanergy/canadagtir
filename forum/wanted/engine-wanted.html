why would it be a weaker motor? the sr20ve head flows far more than a gti-r head, the intake manifold and single throttle body flows more than the gti-r quads. you can run less boost and make equivalent or more power. the only real difference is the head studs on a gti-r block are bigger than the ve/de/det block. plenty of non gti-r sr20's make plenty of power. you can use the gti-r crank in any de/ve, its just a 8cw crank with larger flywheel bolt holes. you can use the gti-r rods on any sr20 crank. Anyways here are the differences
1. standard de/det 8cw crank smaller flywheel bolts, gtir crank is identical larger flywheel bolts. if you use a standard de/det crank, you can use any s13/14 clutch/flywheel.
2. gti-r rods are more beefy using 19mm bearings. all sr20 cranks have the journal room to use gti-r rods.
3. gti-r head has solid lifters and slightly more aggressive cams than other sr20det's. sr20ve has vvl, like vtec 2 profiles with a lot more flow capacity.
4. gti-r has itb's, sr20ve manifold has larger runners and a large single tb.
5. gti-r uses larger head studs.

the ve head is an improvement over the gti-r head. block design really hasn't changed.
