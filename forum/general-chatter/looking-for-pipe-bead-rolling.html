.... a skilled welder might also add some 'stipples' behind the ring where the tube is to be clamped under the rubber for some added grip if required with higher boost.
fwiw: a welder/mechanic buddy at a former vac truck oilfield service company, who routinely installed 10" id. rubber hose on max 15 psi industrial vac/pressure equipment, used to spark a series or grid of rough dots in a couple of helical spirals a bit back of the sealing ring in face of repeated hose blow offs in the field. His method worked. A smooth bead was required for a good seal; the rough stipples of even size and distribution added better grip under the bolted steel clamps.
