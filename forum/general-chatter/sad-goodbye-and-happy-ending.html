A Sad Goodbye... and a Happy Ending

Back at the end of November, my wife and I and a couple of friends went to a fantastic Red Hot Chilli Peppers concert at the MTS Centre (downtown Winnipeg). I had found a great parking spot on the street where I wouldn't have to pay a ridiculous "attraction night" parking fee increase. The show was great, we had a fantastic time, all was well until I got back to the R...


 
Thought it was a hit-and-run, then a bystander pointed me in a different direction...
 

 
Someone had run a red light and hit the rear-passenger side of this Cruze in the intersection, which caused it to spin out and collide with my parked Pulsar. It pushed my car into the curb and partially bent the left (passenger-side) wheel under the car. My car couldn't be driven, so it was towed to the Manitoba Public Insurance (MPI) compound yard. It took over two weeks for my adjuster, along with help from an outside appraiser, to notify me that they were writing off the car with no option to buy it back. They were adament that the car be crushed, as it's RHD and MPI is "trying to get as many off the road as possible"... and also based on the age of the car, telling me their policy is to crush cars older than MY 1995. They let me see my R one more time and pull out my stereo and a few other possesions, where I snapped a few good-bye pics...
 

 

You can see the bent-in rear passnger wheel in this shot.
 
I'm very sad to see it gone. It was a unique and fun car to drive. It was mostly stock with less than 90,000km and only a 3" full exhaust from the turbo (and Walbro), there was so much more I had planned for it. Plus, it was going to be my winter car for years to come so I could pick up a summer-only sports car.
 
Alas, there IS a little bit of a happy ending to all this. After losing my winter car, I wasn't sure what I was going to do about a car for year-round use. I test drove 2 different Audi TT's (AWD, pretty sporty) with the 3.2L VR6 and the magnetic suspension, they were nice but lacked... feel. Too remote, not intuitive enough for my taste.
 
I was 5 minutes away from booking a flight to Toronto to find what I was looking for when I decided to do one last local search... and then I found her: my new Betty...
 

 

 

 
She's been mine for almost a month now, and has helped my grieving heart to move on from the R... plus she's AWD (335Xi) and chipped, so she has the reminiscent thrust and traction of my ol' girl.
 
Thanks for all the advice and interesting topics, GTI-R community! I'll keep trolling around here as a piece of me will always stay with my fast little R.
 
Cheers,
 
Ty
