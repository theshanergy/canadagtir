yea..looks like a tidy example. the thing is...if you are looking for a car that is cheaper than the skyline to maintain then keep looking...this little guy may, at first glance, seem easier to maintain but all the parts are specific to the pulsar. you have to order most of the parts from the uk,..some of the parts come off various other nissans but it takes a bit of leg work to figure it all out. 7500 seems like a good price...make sure you do a fault code check and see if the det sensor and maf and everything is in good order...if you look on this site there is a thread that talks about what to look for when buying one of these cars...take a look and make sure you are not getting ripped off. there are good and very bad examples of these little demons. just make sure you are willing to deal with problems and hick ups along the way.

Just had to mention (in case you didn't notice) that with VIN 821, this is a very early car.
I've worked in the auto mfg industry 12 years now, and the rule of thumb still is not to buy the earliest (bugs are not worked out) or the final (cost-reduced the crap out of it) cars. I usually recommend at least not buying the first or last year. But that said, with only 4 years of production on GTI-R's, there isn't much in between :)

Also, I ran the VIN through FAST. The colour code comes up as TJ1, which I believe is grey. A repaint isn't the worst thing that can happen to a 15 year old car, but the more you know...
