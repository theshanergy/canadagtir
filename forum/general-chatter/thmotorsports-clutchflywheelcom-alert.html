Dear TH Motorsports,

The reasons for my cancellation are as follows:

I own a 1993 Nissan Pulsar GTiR.  My clutch broke and this is my daily driver.  I needed a replacement clutch soon, but I wouldn't settle for anything less than the ACT 6 puck which I have used in the past and loved.  I had never ordered anything from your company before, and stumbled across ClutchFlywheel.  There was advertised the clutch that I wanted, in the kit that I needed, for the price that I was willing to pay.  Also reading on the website is a 99.9% in stock banner.  It was a perfect check list of everything I was looking for.

I ordered this clutch on January 4th, 2012.  Your website states that orders are processed same or next day.

On January 13th, 7 business days after the latest time the order would have been processed I drove 1.5 hours in a snowstorm to Port Huron Michigan to collect my clutch.  When I arrived it was not there.  I was in disbelief.  7 business days to ship from Illinois to Michigan;  unheard of.  I then checked the order status online.  There were no notes or updates of any kind to indicate that the order had been delayed for any reason.  At this point I contacted your shipping department to make an inquiry.  A very helpful employee by the name of Peter, worked for several hours trying to find my order.  When he finally did, he informed me that you do not stock this clutch and that there was some sort of error in the system showing stock.  He also informed me that ACT also does not stock this clutch and would have to build one before sending it out.  This would take until approximately the 23rd before it was even ready to ship.  This would make it impossible for me to fix my car in time to be able to have transportation to work.

I find it utterly incomprehensible that a company advertising 99.9% in stock would not make EVERY possible effort to ensure that their customer was aware of an item that was NOT currently in stock.  This has cost me a significant amount of time and money and I have nothing to show for it.

I will endeavour to alert the very tight knit GTiR community of this episode, so that they may be spared the headache to having to deal with your business.  I fully understand that mistakes can happen.  I am fully aware that no company is perfect, and that there are bound to be mistakes due to the sales volume of a large company.  However, I find it totally irresponsible to leave your customers out to dry when they are spending good money on name brand products.  7 business days is more than enough time to attach a 2 word note to the order; "back ordered".

Many thanks goes out to Peter in shipping who was extremely helpful and deserves recognition for his excellent communication and customer service skills.

For what it is worth, this is the first email I have ever had to write to a company in complaint.  I have never been so royally screwed over.

Please cancel my order so that I may use the money to purchase this kit from another supplier.

Sincerely,

Stephen Robertson
