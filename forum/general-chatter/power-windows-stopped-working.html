Easy check, remove the door panel check for pwr/ground at the window motor- while using the switch.should be two wires going to the motor. Polarity is switched, that's how you get your up and down with only 2 wires. If you have all your signals that verifies the circuit is good and it's the window motor. If you do not have your signals you will have to trace back the circuit from there. Go online get the wiring diagram, you will need it. You can check common areas like where the wires pass through from the door to the body. Opening and closing the door puts stress on old wiring and breaks inside the rubber boot are sometimes common on older vehicles. Sometimes you can give the motor a tap with a hammer and it will start working again( maybe for a long time or maybe just enough to get it back up.) Don't beat on it, just a couple of taps. you might get lucky. With the panel off, now you can inspect the regulator and the run channels, make sure nothing is bent, binding or loose.

So many unsuspecting victims... So little time.

Also... For those of you who have window's that seem to struggle on the way up, lube the glass runs with Silicone spray(AND ONLY SILICONE SPRAY!) The old dried out rubber tends to create a lot of friction against the glass, and may cause binding. Mine were pretty slow when I got my car, one quick spray down each of the channels and they went up as fast as they came down. You may have to repeat once every month or two but it works mint providing the windows are fine otherwise.
