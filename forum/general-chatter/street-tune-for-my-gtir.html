I don't want to go over all the fuel concepts but you won't be adjusting your AFRs per say. Our injectors are set to run at 3 BARS or 43.4psi so our stock non-adjustable FPR is setup to deliver that pressure, the excess fuel pressure is recirculated back into the fuel tank. 

With an adjustable FPR, you can set the static (base) fuel pressure to run. This is usually done via a pressure gauge reading with the car running and no vacuum source hooked up to the FPR. This allows you to run a higher or lower fuel base fuel pressure if desired which in turn affects the entire AFR range. You see, the ECU has been programmed based on a known fact that your injectors are running at 3 BARS delivering 440cc per minute, when you change the base fuel pressure, the ecu has no idea and will keep opening the injectors for x amount of time it is programmed to for each revolution. So for example if you lowered the base fuel pressure, then less fuel is actually injected into the cylinder per rotation.

Now there's the whole thing about 1:1 ratio too. Earlier I mentioned static pressure with no vacuum. When boost is applied, the fuel pressure rises to match the boost pressure increase. here is an example, if you're boosting 10 psi then your overall fuel pressure at that moment will be 43.4 + 10 = 53.4psi of fuel pressure. This is done for ease of tuning and also making it easier to tune for a wide range of boost pressure. I could go on but I think you can read up on the rest of concepts if you're really interested.

I have a book on turbocharging by Corky Bell in PDF format, if you'd like a read then PM me your email and I'll send it to you.

 

In any case, seeing the AFRs at idle in your video and from what you're telling your AFRs are at WOT, it suggests to me that your FPR is dead and needs to be replaced. Many other GTIR owners have had similar issue and resolved by simply replacing this part. Of course it could be an injector o-ring, an injector or your fuel pump but I would start with the FPR as it is likely 20yrs old and will eliminate issues in the future.
