Ok so here is a pic of the ones i got from europe. as long as you have E Code Headlight your ok. And if i remember correctly the taillights on my car also have ecode markings. ECODE Is just as good as DOT. It Will Pass with the E Code markings as they are for lhd roads. DOT is actually us and not Canadian. the cars that come to north america have DOT markings because the states requires it we get the same markings by default. Also i did another thread that tells you how to use 1988-1989 Mazda 323 headlights in place of LHD pulsar lights. They fit very well and the modding is very easy and they fit very well and will pass any Canadian inspection.

http://www.canadagtir.com/forums/canada-gtir/importing/parts-use-so-u-can-pass-dot-inspection

you will have to modify your 323 metal mounting bracket to make it work with the Pulsar but it only takes an hour or so to do it. Also you will want to get the 9004 headlight plug pigtail and some spade connectors to plug into the Pulsar H4 headlight socket. Very easy. If you need the Pics of the modified housing i can dig out the headlights and snap a couple a pics off for you. If i can find the box i might also have the TYC part number for LHD lights. Im not sure tho. Hope this helps. The important part in this pic is the E3 in the circle you need that there to prove there for LHD

I have e-code right now. they are e13.... which is a lhd country, but if you look at the beam pattern its the same as rhd. its the beam pattern that would really matter when push comes to shove realistically, especially in winnipeg with these retarded random inspections.
