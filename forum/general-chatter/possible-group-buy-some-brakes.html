i am, they're cost effective and worth every penny. i have used them over a year through all seaons and they work just fine.
the 11" kit may not hold up to lapping days, but will hold up to any autox or spirited driving.
very little fade if any, can lock up the brakes fast, in my case makes the abs go nutso pretty quick.
but its mainly due to the hps pads that come with the kit kuz they're really just a street pad. change to the rotor eating hp+ and it'd be safe to say it would be outstanding.

i don't know how anyone can drive the gti-r with any confidence with those shit oem brakes. if i stand on the brakes now, i can send the passenger's face into the dashboard. braking from 150km/hr is good with little fade and i'm far more confident in the car's ability to slow down. braking performance from 100km/hr below is pretty amazing.

if you've got big wheels 16 or bigger, i'd go with the 11.75 kit or bigger with the 6piston caliper kit.
i went with the 11" kit to keep my wheelsize down to 15's.

the kit is complete. caliper mounts, calipers, stainless lines, pads, rotors
the 11.75 2 piece rotor is 2lbs lighter than the 11" solid rotor
the caliper is nearly 10lbs less than the oem caliper
pads are super easy to change as it just uses a cotter pin at the top like a 300zx caliper
tons of pad choices with the dynalite caliper
the calipers/pads are a bit squeeky when u get on them, but i could care less, the car itself is loud enough on its own.
you may need to trim back the brake dust shield and grind down the oem caliper bolts a tiny bit as they're a bit long.

anything else?
